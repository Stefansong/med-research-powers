# Revision Strategy

Invoke the `revision-strategy` skill when you receive Major/Minor Revision or Reject decisions.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Classify reviewer comments
- **Critical** — must fix or guaranteed rejection
- **Major** — strongly suggest fixing, method negotiable
- **Minor** — quick fixes, show diligence
- **Disagreeable** — politely rebut with evidence

### Step 2: Prioritize by Impact × Difficulty
High Impact + Low Difficulty → do first. Critical items always first regardless.

### Step 3: Response letter strategy
- Reviewer correct → "We thank the reviewer... We have revised..."
- Reviewer based on misunderstanding → "We may not have been clear enough... We have revised the text..."
- Reviewer clearly wrong → "We respectfully note that [correction with reference]..."
- Reviewer asks impossible experiment → "We agree this would strengthen... Unfortunately [reason]. Added as limitation."

### Step 4: Post-revision re-verification
Re-run `pre-submission-verification` after all changes.

### If Rejected
Improve based on reviewer feedback → `journal-selection` (next tier) → `cover-letter-writing` (reframe) → resubmit.

## Output
`revision-plan.md` — prioritized action list + timeline + response letter framework.
